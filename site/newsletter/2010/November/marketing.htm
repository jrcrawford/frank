 Give Back This November Through Community Relations

This month is all about Thanksgiving. We rush around getting things prepared for the one day we stuff ourselves with food and for the massive shopping day that follows. Hectic times, especially if you have extended family joining you, we all know how those in-laws can be.

But what is Thanksgiving really about? What about those who aren’t able to enjoy Turkey and Pumpkin Pie or hit up the large department store sales on Black Friday?As a business owner, this season gives you the perfect opportunity to give back to your community and gain recognition from your neighborhood and peers. Each month I talk about Public Relations, yet Community Relations is just as important to help project your image to the public and get involved.Although I suggest you partake in volunteering opportunities year round, here are some ideas to get you started this season:

  • Volunteer at a homeless shelter- you can spend a few hours dishing out a yummy Thanksgiving meal to those who need it mostStart a Canned Food Drive- ask everyone in your office to donate a few canned foods, if you have enough people you can even make it a competition, whoever donates the most wins a prize. After you have everything collected donate it to your local food bankVolunteer at a food bank- spend a few hours sorting packaged food to be delivered to those in needAdopt a Family - this gives you the opportunity to provide a full Thanksgiving meal for a less fortunate family

  • Participate in a Fun Run or 5k for charity. Not only will this help those in need, it will help you burn off the calories from all the pie you will enjoy later in the day

From a PR standpoint, the more you broadcast the great things you are doing, the better. Whether you are volunteering at a homeless shelter or participating in a fun run, wear a shirt or hat that has your company logo and talk to everyone you come in contact with. Not only will this help people remember your company but you may just run into someone who has a great business referral. Be sure to announce what you are doing; get friends, family, neighbors and even producers involved. The more the merrier! If you really want to spice it up, try partnering with another agency or a business in your local community to participate in your volunteering efforts. Not only will this strengthen the bonds with others in your community, it will strengthen the bonds you have with your business contacts.

As always, I am here to help with any of your PR and Marketing needs. I wish you a wonderful Thanksgiving! Don’t forget your stretchy pants!
